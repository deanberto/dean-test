Reptile Information Guide

Comprehensive care guides for Large Lizards, Monitors, and Boas

Large Lizards

Blue-Tongued Skink

Overview

Blue-tongued skinks are docile, ground-dwelling lizards known for their distinctive blue tongues and calm temperament. They make excellent pets for both beginners and experienced keepers.

Habitat Requirements

  • Minimum 120cm x 60cm x 60cm enclosure
  • Temperature gradient: 24-28°C cool side, 30-35°C basking spot
  • UVB lighting essential (10-12% UVB)
  • Substrate: Cypress mulch, aspen shavings, or paper towels

Diet

Omnivorous - 60% vegetables/fruits, 40% protein. Feed juveniles daily, adults every 2-3 days.

Legal Status in Ireland

Legal to keep with proper documentation. Check local council regulations.

Bearded Dragon

Overview

Bearded dragons are popular, interactive lizards known for their calm demeanor and distinctive "beard" display. They're excellent for beginners.

Habitat Requirements

  • Minimum 120cm x 60cm x 60cm for adults
  • Basking temperature: 38-42°C
  • Cool side: 24-26°C
  • UVB lighting crucial (10-12% UVB)
  • Substrate: Tile, paper towels, or reptile carpet

Diet

Juveniles: 80% insects, 20% vegetables. Adults: 20% insects, 80% vegetables.

Legal Status in Ireland

Legal to keep. Popular choice for reptile enthusiasts.

Tegu Lizard

Overview

Tegus are large, intelligent lizards that can grow up to 1.2 meters. They require experienced handling and significant space.

Habitat Requirements

  • Minimum 240cm x 120cm x 120cm enclosure
  • Temperature gradient: 24-27°C cool, 35-40°C basking
  • High humidity (60-80%)
  • Deep substrate for burrowing
  • UVB lighting recommended

Diet

Omnivorous with high protein needs. Whole prey, eggs, fruits, and vegetables.

Legal Status in Ireland

Check current regulations - may require special licensing.

Monitor Lizards

Ackie Monitor

Overview

Ackie monitors are smaller monitor species, reaching about 60cm. They're active, intelligent, and suitable for experienced keepers.

Habitat Requirements

  • Minimum 180cm x 90cm x 90cm enclosure
  • Temperature: 26-30°C cool side, 45-50°C basking
  • Low humidity (30-50%)
  • Deep substrate for digging
  • Multiple hiding spots and climbing opportunities

Diet

Carnivorous - insects, small rodents, and occasional eggs. Feed juveniles daily, adults every 2-3 days.

Legal Status in Ireland

May require special licensing. Check with local authorities.

Savannah Monitor

Overview

Savannah monitors are large, robust lizards that can reach 1-1.5 meters. They require experienced handling and extensive space.

Habitat Requirements

  • Minimum 300cm x 150cm x 150cm enclosure
  • Temperature: 27-30°C cool side, 43-49°C basking
  • Moderate humidity (40-60%)
  • Very deep substrate for extensive burrowing
  • Large water area for soaking

Diet

Carnivorous - whole prey items, rodents, and insects. Avoid overfeeding as they're prone to obesity.

Legal Status in Ireland

Likely requires special licensing due to size. Verify current regulations.

Asian Water Monitor

Overview

Asian water monitors are among the largest lizards, reaching 2+ meters. They're for expert keepers only and require massive enclosures.

Habitat Requirements

  • Minimum 600cm x 300cm x 250cm enclosure
  • Large swimming area essential
  • Temperature: 26-30°C cool, 40-45°C basking
  • High humidity (70-90%)
  • Extremely secure enclosure required

Diet

Carnivorous - large prey items, fish, and whole animals. Feeding frequency varies with age.

Legal Status in Ireland

Requires special licensing and permits. Not suitable for most private keepers.

Boa Constrictors

Common Boa Constrictor

Overview

Common boas are impressive snakes that can reach 2-4 meters. They're generally docile but require experienced handling due to their size and strength.

Habitat Requirements

  • Minimum 180cm x 60cm x 60cm for adults
  • Temperature: 24-27°C cool side, 30-32°C warm side
  • Humidity: 50-60%
  • Secure, escape-proof enclosure essential
  • Multiple hiding spots and water bowl

Diet

Carnivorous - appropriately sized rodents. Feed juveniles weekly, adults every 2-3 weeks.

Legal Status in Ireland

Requires special licensing under Dangerous Wild Animals Act. Check local regulations.

Rainbow Boa

Overview

Rainbow boas are beautiful, iridescent snakes known for their stunning colors. They're smaller than common boas but require specific humidity needs.

Habitat Requirements

  • Minimum 120cm x 60cm x 60cm enclosure
  • Temperature: 24-26°C cool side, 29-31°C warm side
  • High humidity: 75-90%
  • Good ventilation to prevent stagnant air
  • Substrate that holds moisture well

Diet

Carnivorous - appropriately sized rodents. Similar feeding schedule to other boas.

Legal Status in Ireland

May require licensing. Verify current regulations before acquisition.

Sand Boa

Overview

Sand boas are smaller, desert-dwelling boas that are excellent for keepers wanting a more manageable boa species.

Habitat Requirements

  • Minimum 90cm x 45cm x 45cm enclosure
  • Temperature: 22-25°C cool side, 30-32°C warm side
  • Low humidity: 30-50%
  • Deep, sandy substrate for burrowing
  • Minimal water requirements

Diet

Carnivorous - small rodents and birds. Feed juveniles weekly, adults every 10-14 days.

Legal Status in Ireland

Generally legal to keep. Check local council regulations.

Breeding Information

Important: Breeding reptiles requires extensive knowledge, proper facilities, and significant commitment. Only experienced keepers should attempt breeding, and all local regulations must be followed.

Large Lizards Breeding

Blue-Tongued Skink Breeding

Sexual Maturity
  • Males: 18-24 months
  • Females: 24-36 months
  • Minimum weight: 400-500g for females
Breeding Season

Spring to early summer (September-December in Australia, March-June in Northern Hemisphere)

Breeding Process
  • Cooling period: 2-3 months at 15-18°C
  • Gradual temperature increase to trigger breeding
  • Gestation: 100-120 days (live birth)
  • Litter size: 5-25 babies
Neonatal Care
  • Separate babies immediately
  • First shed within 1-2 weeks
  • Start feeding after first shed
  • Small insects and finely chopped vegetables

Bearded Dragon Breeding

Sexual Maturity
  • Males: 8-12 months
  • Females: 12-18 months
  • Minimum length: 35cm for females
Breeding Season

Spring (March-May in Northern Hemisphere)

Breeding Process
  • Brumation period: 6-12 weeks at 10-15°C
  • Courtship includes head bobbing and arm waving
  • Egg laying: 15-30 days after mating
  • Clutch size: 15-30 eggs
  • Incubation: 55-75 days at 28-30°C
Egg Care
  • Vermiculite substrate (1:1 ratio by weight with water)
  • Humidity: 80-90%
  • Temperature determines sex: 26-28°C (females), 29-32°C (mixed), 33-35°C (males)

Tegu Lizard Breeding

Sexual Maturity
  • Males: 2-3 years
  • Females: 3-4 years
  • Significant size difference required
Breeding Season

Late spring to early summer (May-July)

Breeding Process
  • Hibernation: 3-4 months at 15-18°C
  • Males become territorial and aggressive
  • Nest building behavior in females
  • Clutch size: 12-30 eggs
  • Incubation: 60-80 days at 28-30°C
Special Considerations
  • Requires very large breeding enclosures
  • Female may become aggressive during nesting
  • High protein diet before breeding season
  • Professional veterinary monitoring recommended

Monitor Lizards Breeding

Ackie Monitor Breeding

Sexual Maturity
  • Males: 18-24 months
  • Females: 24-36 months
  • Minimum SVL: 20cm for females
Breeding Season

Dry season (May-September in captivity)

Breeding Process
  • Cooling period: 6-8 weeks at 22-25°C
  • Reduced photoperiod (8-10 hours)
  • Courtship involves neck biting
  • Clutch size: 3-8 eggs
  • Incubation: 120-180 days at 28-30°C
Nesting Requirements
  • Deep substrate for digging (60cm+)
  • Termite mound substrate preferred
  • High humidity in nesting area (80%+)
  • Multiple nesting sites

Savannah Monitor Breeding

Sexual Maturity
  • Males: 2-3 years
  • Females: 3-4 years
  • Minimum weight: 1.5kg for females
Breeding Season

End of dry season (August-October)

Breeding Process
  • Extended cooling: 8-12 weeks at 20-24°C
  • Dramatic reduction in feeding
  • Males show increased activity and scent marking
  • Clutch size: 15-40 eggs
  • Incubation: 150-200 days at 30°C
Advanced Requirements
  • Massive enclosures (room-sized)
  • Professional-grade incubation systems
  • Veterinary monitoring essential
  • Significant legal considerations

Asian Water Monitor Breeding

Sexual Maturity

  • Females: 4-5 years
  • Minimum length: 1.2m for females
Breeding Season

Wet season (varies by region)

Breeding Process
  • Complex environmental cycling required
  • Seasonal temperature and humidity changes
  • Clutch size: 10-40 eggs
  • Incubation: 180-300 days
Expert Level Only
  • Requires zoo-level facilities
  • Professional breeding programs only
  • Extensive legal requirements

Boa Constrictors Breeding

Common Boa Constrictor Breeding

Sexual Maturity
  • Males: 18-24 months (4+ feet)
  • Females: 3-4 years (6+ feet)
  • Weight more important than age
Breeding Season

Winter months (December-March)

Breeding Process
  • Cooling period: 8-12 weeks at 18-22°C
  • Reduced photoperiod (8 hours)
  • Males develop breeding spurs
  • Gestation: 100-120 days
  • Litter size: 10-60 babies (live birth)
Pregnancy Care
  • Maintain optimal temperatures (26-30°C)
  • Provide secure, stress-free environment
  • Monitor for pre-birth shed
  • Prepare individual housing for babies

Rainbow Boa Breeding

Sexual Maturity
  • Males: 2-3 years
  • Females: 3-4 years
  • Minimum weight: 800g for females
Breeding Season

Dry season (May-August)

Breeding Process
  • Cooling period: 6-8 weeks at 20-24°C
  • Humidity cycling important
  • Males show increased activity
  • Gestation: 120-150 days
  • Litter size: 8-25 babies
Humidity Management
  • Maintain 75-85% humidity during breeding
  • Increase to 90% during gestation
  • Proper ventilation essential
  • Monitor for respiratory issues

Sand Boa Breeding

Sexual Maturity
  • Males: 12-18 months
  • Females: 18-24 months
  • Smaller species mature faster
Breeding Season

Spring (March-May)

Breeding Process
  • Cooling period: 6-10 weeks at 15-20°C
  • Reduced feeding during cooling
  • Males may refuse food during breeding
  • Gestation: 90-120 days
  • Litter size: 5-20 babies
Desert Species Considerations
  • Lower humidity requirements (30-50%)
  • Temperature drops more dramatic
  • Seasonal lighting changes important
  • Substrate depth crucial for nesting behavior

General Breeding Guidelines

Pre-Breeding Preparation

Health Assessment
  • Veterinary examination for both animals
  • Parasite screening and treatment
  • Weight and body condition assessment
  • Genetic screening if available
Facility Requirements
  • Separate quarantine facilities
  • Multiple breeding enclosures
  • Incubation equipment (for egg-laying species)
  • Nursery setups for offspring

Legal and Ethical Considerations

Legal Requirements
  • Check local and national breeding regulations
  • Obtain necessary permits and licenses
  • Understand species-specific restrictions
  • Plan for offspring placement
Ethical Responsibilities
  • Only breed healthy, unrelated animals
  • Have homes lined up for offspring
  • Avoid contributing to overpopulation
  • Maintain detailed breeding records

Record Keeping

Essential Records
  • Breeding dates and observations
  • Incubation temperatures and humidity
  • Hatch/birth dates and success rates
  • Individual animal tracking
Health Documentation
  • Veterinary examination reports
  • Feeding records and growth charts
  • Any health issues or treatments
  • Genetic information and lineage

General Care Tips

Research First

Always thoroughly research any species before acquisition. Understand their full adult size, lifespan, and care requirements.

Legal Compliance

Check all local and national regulations. Some species require special licenses or may be prohibited in certain areas.

Veterinary Care

Establish a relationship with an exotic veterinarian before you need one. Regular health checks are essential.

Quarantine

Always quarantine new animals for at least 30-60 days before introducing them to your collection.
